        <h1>History of Flat Design</h1> <p>Flat Design has evolved from earlier design principles into the popular aesthetic we see today. Here's a brief timeline:</p> <div class="timeline"> <div class="timeline-item"> <div class="timeline-dot"></div> <div class="timeline-content"> <h3>1940s: Swiss Style</h3> <p>Dominates the scene with grids, sans-serif typography, and clean hierarchy. Heavily influenced by minimalism.</p> </div> </div> <div class="timeline-item"> <div class="timeline-dot"></div> <div class="timeline-content"> <h3>2006: Microsoft</h3> <p>Releases Zune media player - large, lowercase typography and background imagery.</p> </div> </div> <div class="timeline-item"> <div class="timeline-dot"></div> <div class="timeline-content"> <h3>2010: Microsoft</h3> <p>Releases Windows Phone 7 - large, bright grids, sans-serif, flat icons. Dubbed "Metro" design.</p> </div> </div> <div class="timeline-item"> <div class="timeline-dot"></div> <div class="timeline-content"> <h3>2012: Microsoft</h3> <p>Releases Windows 8 with Metro design - block grid layout, sharp edges, bright colours and large background images.</p> </div> </div> <div class="timeline-item"> <div class="timeline-dot"></div> <div class="timeline-content"> <h3>2013: Apple</h3> <p>Releases iOS 7 - flat design shakes off previous skeuomorphic approach. Skeuomorphism across the web quickly becomes outdated.</p> </div> </div> </div>
